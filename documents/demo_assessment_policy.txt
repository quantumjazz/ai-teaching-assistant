Assessment Policy

This synthetic demo document defines the grading policy for the hosted AI teaching assistant demo. The final grade combines four components: individual case analysis, group facilitation, final reflection, and participation.

Individual case analysis is worth 40 percent of the final grade. Students receive a short business case, identify the decision problem, list two or three feasible alternatives, choose a heuristic method, and justify a recommendation with evidence from the case.

Group facilitation is worth 30 percent of the final grade. Teams lead a workshop using one method from the course, such as Delphi, scenario analysis, decision trees, or weighted scoring. A strong facilitation session makes assumptions visible and records dissenting views.

The final reflection is worth 20 percent of the final grade. Students explain where a heuristic helped, where it could mislead, and what information would be needed before turning the recommendation into an operational plan.

Participation is worth 10 percent of the final grade. Participation credit comes from concise preparation notes, useful questions during workshops, and respectful critique of other teams' assumptions.

Late work can receive partial credit for up to seven calendar days after the deadline. After seven days, the student must request an instructor exception and explain the blocker.
